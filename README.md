# GaitHub

### A digital health companion designed to democratize digital mobility biomarker analytics

GaitHub is an emerging digital health platform designed to make advanced mobility analysis accessible, interpretable, and clinically meaningful in healthy and pathological aging.

Modern wearable sensors can capture an extraordinary amount of movement data during daily life. Yet transforming these raw signals into clinically interpretable Digital Mobility Outcomes (DMOs) remains technically complex and largely inaccessible outside specialized research laboratories. This gap is widely recognized as one of the central barriers preventing the large-scale adoption of wearable mobility biomarkers in clinical practice.

GaitHub was created to help close that gap. The platform aims to democratize access to state-of-the-art wearable mobility analytics by providing intuitive, standardized, and scientifically grounded tools that allow users to process, visualize, and interpret mobility data without requiring expertise in signal processing, biomechanics, coding, or data science.

---

# Why GaitHub

Wearable mobility biomarkers have the potential to transform healthcare by enabling:
- earlier detection of mobility decline,
- objective monitoring of disease progression,
- personalized care,
- and remote monitoring.

However, extracting clinically meaningful metrics from wearable sensor data often requires:
- advanced programming skills,
- specialized signal processing knowledge,
- complex preprocessing pipelines,
- and familiarity with highly technical research software.

For many clinicians, healthcare professionals, students, researchers, rehabilitation specialists, industry users, and patients, these requirements create a major accessibility barrier. GaitHub is designed to help remove that barrier.

---

# Designed for Non-Experts

GaitHub is specifically built for users with little or no technical expertise in wearable data analysis.

Potential non-expert users include:
- physiotherapists,
- neurologists,
- geriatricians,
- kinesiologists,
- occupational therapists,
- podiatrists,
- nurses,
- clinical coordinators,
- exercise professionals,
- researchers,
- trainees,
- and patients.

The platform provides a streamlined workflow that allows users to move from raw wearable data to interpretable DMOs capable of supporting clinical decision-making.

# Also Built for Experts

While accessibility is a core mission of GaitHub, the platform is equally intended to support expert users.

GaitHub aims to provide:
- standardized processing pipelines,
- reproducible workflows,
- transparent methodologies,
- scalable analyses,
- and easier integration across research projects and clinical studies.

---

# Scientific Foundation

GaitHub is developed around state-of-the-art processing pipelines and algorithms validated in the scientific literature.

The platform is being designed with a strong emphasis on interpretability and usability. The long-term vision is not simply to compute movement metrics, but to help users better understand what these metrics actually mean in the context of human mobility in healthy and pathological aging.

---

# Current Pre-Release Version

The current pre-release version of GaitHub is intentionally focused and limited in scope.

At this stage, the platform:
- focuses specifically on Parkinson’s disease,
- is currently available only on Windows,
- computes a restricted set of gait-related DMOs,
- and is currently designed around data acquired using the APDM (Ambulatory Parkinson’s Disease Monitoring) Opal wearable sensor system.

The present version primarily serves as an initial foundation for future large-scale development.

---

# Vision for the First Full Release

The first full release of GaitHub will substantially expand the platform’s capabilities.

Planned developments include:
- a much larger library of validated DMOs,
- secure data management and privacy-focused infrastructure,
- support for additional wearable sensing modalities,
- deployment across multiple operating systems and devices (e.g., tablets, smartphones, and web browsers),
- broader applicability across healthy and pathological aging populations,
- integration into existing clinical workflows,
- and interoperability with digital health ecosystems.

Future versions are also planned to include intelligent agent-based assistance systems capable of helping users:
- better interpret outputs,
- understand mobility patterns,
- identify potential abnormalities and uncertainties,
- generate automated summaries,
- and navigate analyses through conversational interfaces.

These features aim to further democratize wearable mobility analytics and make advanced movement analysis accessible far beyond specialized laboratories.
